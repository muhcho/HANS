Andersen’s Childhood House

Open Google Maps: Munkemøllestræde 3, 5000 Odense C

From his childhood abode, Hans Christian Andersen glimpsed the world, where the warm embrace of summer and the harsh chill of winter unfolded before his eyes. It was within these walls that, amidst the somberness of his father's passing, in the depths of poverty, the treasures of his heart and the vast expanse of his imagination remained untouched. It was within these modest confines that Hans Christian discovered the wellspring of inspiration for his tales and the realms of his dreams.

Accessibility at H.C. Andersen Childhood House is designed to accommodate wheelchair users and individuals with disabilities, ensuring easy and convenient access throughout the premises.

For individuals with hearing impairments: Visitors who are hard of hearing may find that their experience is somewhat limited. Currently, the option to receive a printed copy of the audio track is not available, and the Visioguide is only offered in Danish.

Personal care assistants working with visitors are entitled to free admission to the museum. Please present your care assistant ID card for verification.

There are accessible toilet facilities.

Included with your ticket:

With a ticket to H.C. Andersen’s Childhood House, you can enjoy a 50% discount on admission to the Carl Nielsen Museum, Møntergården, or The Funen Village within 8 days from your visit.

Food and Beverage Policy:

Please note that visitors are not allowed to bring their own food and beverages into the museum. If you feel hungry or crave a cup of coffee, feel free to visit our on-site café.

Luggage Policy:

Large bags and rucksacks are not permitted inside the museum.

Smoking policy:

Smoking is strictly prohibited within the museum premises.

Pets policy:

Unfortunately, pets are not permitted in the museum. However, assistance dogs are more than welcome.

The charming half-timbered house, painted in a soft yellow hue, is nestled in Munkemøllestræde. It was the cherished home of Hans Christian Andersen and his parents during his formative years, from the age of 2 to 14. At 14, he ventured to Copenhagen to pursue his aspirations at the Royal Danish Theatre. Comprising three quaint rooms, the house served as the backdrop for Hans Christian Andersen's blossoming imagination and dreams.
