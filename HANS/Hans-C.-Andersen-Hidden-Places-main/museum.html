Accessibility at H.C. Andersen House is designed to accommodate wheelchair users and individuals with disabilities, ensuring easy and convenient access throughout the premises.

For individuals with hearing impairments: Visitors who are hard of hearing may find that their experience is somewhat limited. Currently, the option to receive a printed copy of the audio track is not available, and the Visioguide is only offered in Danish.

For individuals with visual impairments: The exhibition heavily relies on visual elements, constituting a significant part of the overall experience. Individuals with visual disabilities should anticipate a somewhat diminished experience. Nonetheless, even with visual impairments, you can enjoy a delightful tour of H.C. Andersen House, for instance, by listening to the audio track.

Personal care assistants working with visitors are entitled to free admission to the museum. Please present your care assistant ID card for verification.

There are lifts and accessible toilet facilities.

Included with your ticket:

With a ticket to H.C. Andersen’s House, you can enjoy a 50% discount on admission to the Carl Nielsen Museum, Møntergården, or The Funen Village within 8 days from your visit to H.C. Andersen’s House.

Food and Beverage Policy:

Please note that visitors are not allowed to bring their own food and beverages into the museum. If you feel hungry or crave a cup of coffee, feel free to visit our on-site café.

Luggage Policy:

Large bags and rucksacks are not permitted inside the museum.

Smoking policy:

Smoking is strictly prohibited within the museum premises.

Pets policy:

Unfortunately, pets are not permitted in the museum. However, assistance dogs are more than welcome.

Address: H.C. Andersen Haven 1, 5000 Odense C

Explore the unexpected and captivating world of Hans Christian Andersen's House, where a visit unfolds as a journey into the author's creative universe and the enchanting realm of his globally celebrated fairy tales. Imagination seamlessly intertwines with beauty in the museum's world-class architecture, inviting nature indoors and prompting contemplation about the present and future from unconventional perspectives. Through a groundbreaking exhibition, 12 international artists collaborate to design sound, light, space, and scenery, providing a unique experience where Andersen and his works come alive, speaking directly to each visitor.

From the moment you enter the building, the exhibition transports you away from the familiar, drawing you even deeper into the enchanting realm of Hans Christian Andersen’s fairy tales. Here, everything we think we know reveals new facets of itself. Throughout this captivating journey, the architecture harmonizes with magical gardens, crafting a unique experience propelled by unparalleled creative genius and humor. Every facet of Hans Christian Andersen’s House holds a narrative, and you become an integral part of that captivating story.
